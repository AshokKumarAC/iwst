    Profile

    Dr Manoj Dubey is a wood technocrat with research, teaching and outreach experience in the field of bio-resource processing technology including bamboo in different academic/research positions in more than 3 countries.

    Currently, he is working as Scientist F, Wood Properties and Processing Division and as Head Extension of IWST.

    Earlier, he has worked as Joint Director, IPIRTI Bengaluru (2016-22); Post-Doctoral Researcher, Pennsylvania State University, USA (2013-15); Assistant Professor Forestry, GGV-Central University (2012-13); Assistant Professor Wood Technology, Adama University (2010-12); Lab Demonstrator CAPE, University of Canterbury NZ (2006-10), Research Associate IIFM Bhopal (2004-05) and Research Fellow, FRI Dehradun (2001-02).

    Research Activities

    Improvement in properties of non-traditional low value resources is a significant issue to meet the growing demand of wood and wood products in a sustainable way. The applications of such materials and significance of improved processing with minimum adverse impact on human health and environment have been growing around the world. Thus, my research interest is focused on development of sustainable technologies for non-traditional and low value bio-resources such as bamboo such as development of dielectric heating as a sustainable bio-resource technology; using heat-treatment for value addition of bio-resources and development of bio-composites from low value non-traditional resources such as bamboo, bio-residues.
